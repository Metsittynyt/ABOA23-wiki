Problem

Finland is a land of many marshlands. These marshlands have been drained overtime to use for agriculture and forestry. As these marshlands have been ditched, the methyl mercury that has bioaccumulated to the biomass for centuries, is released to nearby water bodies in the runoff water.

Why is mercury bad?

Mercury is a toxic heavy metal that damages lungs, kidneys, skin and eyes and causes individuals to have weakened immune system, problems with their digestive system and damaged nervous system. It also harms the development of young children and fetuses.

Mercury can be found naturally from the atmosphere, bodies of water and soil. The most toxic form of mercury is methyl mercury. It binds into organic substances and enriches in the foodchains. Methyl mercury causes behavioral, neurological and reproductive issues in animals. Humans get exposed to methyl mercury most often by consuming large predator fish and crustaceans that have high concentrations of methyl mercury.

Numbers about mercury

"Almost all people in the world have at least trace amounts of methylmercury in their tissues, reflecting its pervasive presence in the environment.” - United States Environmental Protection Agency

In 2022, an estimated 2,200 metric tons of mercury was produced worldwide. Mercury is mainly used in the manufacturing of electrical and electronic goods as well as industrial chemicals. - M. Garside, Mar 21, 2023, Statista

Weekly tolerable intake of methyl mercury is 1.3 μg/kg - The European Food Safety Authority (EFSA)

Developing children and youth and fertile women should eat large fish only 1 or 2 times a month - Finnish Food Authority

Artisanal and small-scale gold mining (ASGM) uses 892 tonnes of mercury as a median value yearly and emmits 615 tonnes of mercury as a median value - Cheng, Y., et al 2023

Current solutions

The current solutions to remove mercury from water is to filter it. There are various methods to filter mercury such as reverse osmosis filters and water distillers which both require a lot of energy. Chemical methods such as coagulation uses aluminum sulfate to produce a sludge with ionic mercury. This method is inexpensive but is not sustainable as aluminum sulfate has to be produced for it.


Yet, none of these solutions filter methyl mercury. The bond between the ionic mercury and the methyl molecule has to be broken in order to collect the mercury.

Our solution

To battle this challenge, we have come up with a practically free-of-charge -solution that is also a carbon sink! As our host organism, we use photoautotrophic micro-organisms called cyanobacteria. These microbes use only light and carbondioxide to function, which makes our solution sustainable and scalable.

MercuLess

In our solution, MercuLess, purifies waters from methyl mercury pollution with the means of synthetic biology.

We break the bond between the methyl group and the mercury with a merA gene.

Then using a merB gene, the ionic mercury is transformed into less toxic elemental mercury, which can be collected from the bioreactor.

Cyanobacteria

As these photoautotrophic bacteria use only light as their energy and carbon dioxide as their source of carbon to function, they hold endless possibilities for sustainable solutions in all sectors of industry. Because no external and expensive sugars are needed, no restrictions of energy or carbon are faced in the process. Also, no complex mediums are needed as they naturally can grow in both saline or fresh water!

Cyanobacteria also have a native merA gene, which makes them a natural choice for MercuLess.

Our Goals

Our goal is to purify natural and processed waters from methyl mercury pollution by using genetically modified cyanobacteria. Removing methyl mercury from these waters will support ecological balances, enable the safe use of fish and provide clean habitats.

Safe Habitat

Animals, wildlife and humans, suffer from mercury. Methylmercury accumulates to various tissues and interferes with the metabolic activities causing necrosis and degeneration in them

Ecosystem

The wellbeing and safety of organisms has a direct influence on entire ecosystems: every organism has its own role and harming just one can have major effects on the whole system. Also by protecting ecosystems we preserve biodiversity.

Food Security

We know that the future holds threats to food security therefore it’s important to ensure that our waters are clean from mercury so that we can use fish and other seafoods safely.
