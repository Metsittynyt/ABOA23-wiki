In order to use Synechocystis to fight methylmercury, it needs gene called merB. merB codes for MerB protein which is a organomercury lyase. It catalyses the protonolysis of the bond between methyl’s carbon and mercury.

After the reaction mercury is still in its ionic 2+ form. Another gene called merA is needed for the production of MerA. MerA is a reductase that catalyses the reduction of mercury 2+ ion to elemental mercury.

To enhance and control the expression of these MerB and MerA proteins we will use a strong promoter that we can control with IPTG. Elemental mercury leaves the cell due to diffusion and it’s then gathered up.

Our Goals

We, team ABOA, are tackling this mercury problem to ensure clean and safe habitat for organisms, to protect ecosystems and secure safe use of fish and other food sources. Read more on these by clicking banners below!

Safe Habitat

Animals, wildlife and humans, suffer from mercury. Methylmercury accumulates to various tissues and interferes with the metabolic activities causing necrosis and degeneration in them

Ecosystem

The wellbeing and safety of organisms has a direct influence on entire ecosystems: every organism has its own role and harming just one can have major effects on the whole system. Also by protecting ecosystems we preserve biodiversity.

Food Security

We know that the future holds threats to food security therefore it’s important to ensure that our waters are clean from mercury so that we can use fish and other seafoods safely.
